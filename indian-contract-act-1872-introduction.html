Essentials of a Valid Contract

  • Two Parties: Both Natural and Artificial persons. Exception: State of Gujarat vs. Ramanlal S and Co.
  • Legal Relationship: Intent to create legal obligations. Exception: Balfour v. Balfour (1919) - Domestic agreements are generally not contracts.
  • Legal Formalities: Must be fulfilled, e.g., using stamp paper for immovable properties.
  • Certainty: The object and consideration must be clear and certain.
  • Possibility of Performance: The act must be possible to perform.
  • Legal Object: The purpose of the agreement must be lawful.
  • Lawful Consideration: The "something in return" must be lawful.
  • Competency: Parties must be capable of contracting (not minors, of sound mind, etc.).
  • Free Consent: Consent must not be obtained by coercion, fraud, etc.
  • Agreement Not Expressly Declared Void: The agreement must not be one which the law declares to be void.

Types of Contracts (By Validity)

Valid: An agreement which is binding and enforceable.
Void: A contract which ceases to be enforceable by law. (Was legal initially).
Voidable: Enforceable at the option of one party but not at the option of the other.
Illegal: A contract which the law forbids to be made.
Unenforceable: A contract which is good in substance but cannot be enforced due to a technical defect.

Types of Contracts (By Formation)

  • Express Contract: Terms are expressed by words spoken or written.
  • Implied Contract: Inferred from the actions, conduct, circumstances, or dealings of the parties. Example: Tacit Contract (withdrawing cash from an ATM).
  • Quasi Contract: An obligation created by law, not by agreement, to prevent unjust enrichment.
  • E-Contract: Entered into using electronic means, like email or other electronic data interchange.

Types of Contracts (By Performance)

  • Executed Contract: Where both parties have performed their respective obligations.
  • Executory Contract: Where the obligations are to be performed in the future.
    • Unilateral: Only one party has to perform their obligation.
    • Bilateral: Both parties have to perform their obligations.
